Tactful redaction

Welcome to Dactful

Get an AI's help on real docs without handing it your private information.

1. Why it matters

Tools like Gemini, ChatGPT, and Claude can retain and train on what you paste in unless you have explicitly opted out, and even then, sensitive details are best kept out of a third party's hands. Dactful lets you swap out the parts you would rather not share before you paste, so you keep the AI's usefulness without the exposure.

To be clear, Dactful is not part of those tools and does not make them private. Anything you upload to an AI still travels over the internet and can be stored on its servers. Dactful only removes sensitive details from your text or document first, so you decide what actually leaves your machine.

2. How it works

Say you want Gemini to sharpen your resume, but it lists your home address, personal email, and current employer.

  1. Drop your resume into the Redact tab.
  2. Dactful flags potentially sensitive information and lets you confirm what to hide. Add anything it missed.
  3. You get a clean version where those details become neutral labels like [[EMAIL_1]] or [[COMPANY_1]].
  4. Paste that into Gemini and iterate freely. It never sees the real details.
  5. When the wording is final, drop it into the Restore tab and Dactful swaps your real information back in, ready to use.
  6. If you head back to Gemini to keep iterating, make sure you paste in a redacted version, not the restored one.

3. You're the ultimate reviewer of your data

Dactful catches the obvious cases and the common patterns, but always skim the redacted copy before it leaves your machine.

Welcome to Dactful

Dactful lets you use AI chatbots (like Claude or ChatGPT) without handing over your private information.

1. Why use this?

AI chatbots can learn from what you type into them, unless you turn that setting off. And even when a company says your data is safe, it is not a good idea to paste in private things like your name, address, or account numbers. Dactful hides those first, so you still get the AI's help without giving away your details.

Keep in mind: Dactful is not built into Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini. Whatever you send to a chatbot still travels over the internet and can be stored on that company's computers. Dactful only takes the private details out of your document first, so you choose what actually gets sent.

2. How to use it

Say you got a confusing power bill and you want Claude to explain it.

  1. On the Redact tab, add your power bill.
  2. Dactful points out potentially sensitive information, like your name and home address. Tick the ones you want to hide.
  3. Press the button. You get a clean copy where those parts are swapped for labels like [[PERSON_1]].
  4. Give that clean copy to Claude and ask your questions. Claude never sees your real name or address.
  5. When Claude replies, open the Restore tab, paste the reply, and Dactful puts your real details back to make it easy to read.
  6. If you want to go back to Claude to keep asking questions, make sure you use a redacted version of your document.

3. Always double-check

Before you send anything to an AI, read it over and make sure every private detail is truly gone. Dactful does the heavy lifting, but you are the final check.

A few words, explained

Redact: hide private information by swapping it for a label.

Label (or tag): a stand-in like [[PERSON_1]] that takes the place of a real name or number.

Restore: put your real information back once the AI is done.
